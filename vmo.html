Who We Are

A Living Archive
of Highland Bhutan

The RTC Heritage Portal was established as a collaborative research and documentation initiative under Royal Thimphu College. We bring together students, scholars, and highland communities to co-create a rich, multilayered record of Bhutan's intangible cultural heritage — before it fades from living memory. Our work is guided by three foundational pillars: a clear vision for the future, a purposeful mission for the present, and concrete objectives that shape every project and partnership we undertake.

01 Vision

A World That Remembers

We envision a future in which the living traditions, oral histories, and ecological wisdom of Bhutan's highland peoples are honoured, accessible, and continuously renewed — not as museum artefacts, but as breathing, practised culture. Our vision is of communities empowered by their own heritage, and a global audience enriched by the depth of what the Himalayan highlands hold.

02 Mission

Document. Preserve. Amplify.

Our mission is to systematically document the intangible cultural heritage of highland Bhutan through rigorous fieldwork, participatory storytelling, and digital archiving — ensuring that knowledge held by community elders reaches scholars, students, and future generations alike. We strive to do this with respect, accuracy, and community consent at the centre of every undertaking.

03 Objectives

Grounded in Purposeful Action

From training student researchers to building open-access digital archives, our objectives translate our mission into tangible results. Each semester, new cohorts of RTC students enter the highlands with recording equipment and research frameworks, returning with materials that are processed, annotated, and published through this portal for public and academic use.

Our Objectives
in Detail

Six guiding objectives that define how we work — and why the work matters for the highland communities we serve alongside.

01

Community-Led Documentation

Engage highland communities as active co-creators — not passive subjects — in every stage of the documentation process, from project design through to final publication and archiving.

Core
02

Student Research Capacity

Train and deploy Royal Thimphu College students in ethnographic fieldwork methodologies, oral history recording, photographic documentation, and responsible data management practices.

Education
03

Open Digital Archive

Build and maintain a freely accessible digital repository of films, photo essays, ethnographic vignettes, and oral history recordings — structured for both academic citation and general public engagement.

Digital
04

Intergenerational Knowledge Transfer

Facilitate structured knowledge-sharing sessions between highland elders and younger generations, ensuring traditional practices — from medicinal plant knowledge to festival rituals — are documented and transmitted.

Heritage
05

Regional & International Collaboration

Partner with universities, cultural institutions, and heritage organisations across South Asia and beyond to share methodologies, co-publish research, and broaden the reach of Bhutanese highland heritage internationally.

Global
06

Policy Advocacy for Cultural Preservation

Use research findings to inform national cultural policy and advocate for dedicated resources for intangible heritage preservation within Bhutan's broader development agenda and GNH framework.

Policy

Four Pillars of Our Approach

Every project and partnership is guided by these foundational working principles.

🤝

Ethical Fieldwork

All research is conducted with full informed consent, community review of outputs before publication, and a commitment to cultural sensitivity and accuracy. Communities retain the right to withdraw or restrict any materials collected within their territories.

01
📖

Academic Rigour

Our documentation follows established ethnographic and oral history methodologies, with all materials peer-reviewed by faculty researchers and community liaisons before entering the archive. Citation standards are maintained throughout.

02
🌏

Open Access

Knowledge belongs to everyone. All non-sensitive materials in the portal are freely available under a Creative Commons licence, enabling researchers, educators, filmmakers, and members of the public to access and build upon our archive.

03
🏔️

Place-Based Thinking

Every project is rooted in a specific highland landscape — Haa, Sephu, Laya, Lunana. We believe that culture cannot be separated from the land that shaped it, and our documentation always situates knowledge within its ecological and geographic context.
