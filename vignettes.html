Small Portraits of a Vast Cultural World

Ethnographic vignettes are short, closely observed accounts of everyday moments — a lamp being lit, a story told over butter tea, a skill passed from elder to child. Together they form a mosaic of highland Bhutanese life: ordinary, sacred, and irreplaceable.

Films Vignettes Photo Essay Lesson Plans

The Butter Lamp Room

A written sketch of an elderly nun who tends 108 lamps each morning in a monastery above Trongsa — alone, methodical, luminous.

When the Snow Comes Early

A Laya herder recounts the winter of 2019 — when three feet of unexpected snow buried pastureland and changed a migration route forever.

Ara & Absence

On the ritual of distilling home-brewed ara and the conversations it opens — a vignette about grief, memory, and highland hospitality.

The Archery Ground at Dusk

A single afternoon at a village datse competition — laughter, rivalry, and the unspoken language between men who have known each other all their lives.

Reading the Sky

An elder from Merak explains cloud formations, wind direction, and snowfall patterns — knowledge passed down without ever being written.

The Last Thangka Painter

A portrait of a master thangka artist in his seventies — his hands still steady, his apprentice absent, his pigments ground from stone.
