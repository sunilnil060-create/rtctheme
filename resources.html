Historic Trade Routes of Northern Bhutan

Digitised and georeferenced maps of the salt and wool trade routes connecting the Tibetan plateau to lowland Bhutan, overlaid with contemporary satellite imagery and annotated oral route descriptions from highland elders.

Oral History Collection — Laya Gewog

Over 14 hours of recorded oral histories from Layap elders, covering origin myths, seasonal migration narratives, yak herding practices, and the oral poetry tradition of the Brokpa community. Available in Layap dialect with Dzongkha transcripts.

Partner Institutions & External Links

Trusted organisations whose archives, databases, and programmes complement and extend the work of the RTC Heritage Portal.

01

Bhutan Department of Culture

Official repository of national heritage policy, intangible heritage registries, and cultural preservation programmes across all 20 dzongkhags.

↗ 02

UNESCO Intangible Heritage — Asia-Pacific

Regional programmes and best-practice frameworks for safeguarding intangible cultural heritage across the Himalayan region, including Bhutan's submissions.

↗ 03

Khesar Gyalpo University of Medical Sciences

Partner institution for traditional medicine documentation, with shared research on medicinal plant use and indigenous health practices in highland communities.

↗ 04

National Library & Archives of Bhutan

Home to thousands of religious manuscripts, historical records, and rare Dzongkha texts — many of which are directly relevant to highland cultural history.

↗ 05

Himalayan Art Resources (HAR)

International database of Buddhist and Himalayan art, iconography, and thangka painting traditions — a valuable reference for RTC's visual culture research.

↗ 06

Ugyen Wangchuck Institute for Conservation

Research partner for projects at the intersection of biodiversity conservation and traditional ecological knowledge across Bhutan's northern protected areas.
