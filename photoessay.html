Visual Stories From the Roof of the World

These photo essays document the textures, rhythms, and quiet moments of highland Bhutanese life. Captured by researchers and local photographers, each series moves beyond surface beauty — offering an intimate look at ritual, labour, kinship, and the landscape that shapes everything.

Films Photo Essay Vignettes Lesson Plans

Before the Mask

Backstage portraits of cham dancers preparing in silence — robes folded, faces calm before transformation.

Above the Cloud Line

High-altitude landscapes of the Lunana Gewog, where glacial lakes reflect a sky untouched by light pollution.

Fire & Juniper

The lhab-sang smoke ritual at dawn — photographs of incense, prayer flags, and ash rising over mountain ridgelines.

Felt & Flock

The craft of namda felt-making in Haa — hands pressing raw wool into dense, patterned mats beside open hearths.

The Harvest Table

Post-harvest gatherings in Phobjikha valley — red rice, ara, and communal meals laid on woven grass mats.

Daughters of Sephu

Young women from Sephu balancing school notebooks and traditional striped kiras — two worlds in a single frame.
